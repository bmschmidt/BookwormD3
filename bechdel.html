Movie Language, genre, and the Bechdel Test

This interactive chart lets you explore the words used used in about a thousand movies by genre and, optionally, by score on the Bechdel test.

The dataset is the same as on my time chart of language in movies, but here the emphasis is on browsing by genre instead.





How?

This is all implemented using the D3 visualization layer on the core Bookworm library.

The scores from bechdeltest.com and are graded on the Bechdel Test by the community and also uploaded as srt files from open subtitles.

Why?

Because we need to really start expanding ways of using Bookworm for things other than line charts. I'm using it to flesh out some ideas for implementing the D3-based Bookworm plugin to produce things other than line charts.

When you use the Bechdel test, this is a pretty arcane statistic, to be clear; you're not looking at the percentages of movies passing the test here (which I could show, but is covered), but rather the percentage of terms in the search box. But I'm including it as an example of how to build a bootstrap-based bookworm browser on any corpus at all.

If you've already got a Bookworm running on a corpus of your own and want help implementing this on your own sources, get in touch.
